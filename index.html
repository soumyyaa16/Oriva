How Oriva Calculates Risk

Oriva uses a rule-based clinical decision support formula inspired by AI triage systems. Each patient is scored dynamically based on three parameters.

risk_score =
(age × 0.3) +
(comorbidities × 20) +
(100 − oxygen_level)
HIGHScore above 70 — immediate attention
MODERATEScore 40–70 — monitor closely
LOWScore below 40 — stable
What Are Drug Interactions?

A drug-drug interaction (DDI) occurs when two or more drugs affect each other's activity. This can increase toxicity, reduce effectiveness, or cause entirely new harmful effects.

DDIs are a leading cause of preventable hospital admissions in India. Studies show that over 67% of prescriptions with 3+ drugs contain at least one potential interaction.

Oriva detects these automatically at the point of prescription — before the patient takes the drug.

Why Generic Alternatives Matter

Brand-name drugs and their generic equivalents contain the same active ingredients and are therapeutically equivalent. However, brand drugs can cost 3–10x more than generics.

In India, the average patient spends 30–40% of their income on medicines. Oriva's finance layer identifies safer generic alternatives that reduce cost without compromising clinical outcomes.

Our system has identified alternatives saving patients between 42% and 85% per prescription.

About Oriva

Oriva is a DBMS-driven Prescription Intelligence & Safety Engine designed to address a critical gap in Indian hospital infrastructure.

While standalone drug interaction checkers exist globally, Indian hospitals lack integrated systems that embed safety checks directly into the prescription workflow.

Oriva combines clinical safety, AI-inspired risk scoring, and financial optimization into one unified platform — making it a complete decision support system for modern healthcare.
